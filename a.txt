The two models
MODEL A — Self-Serve SaaS (what we've been building)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Business signs up → logs into YOUR dashboard →
uploads their own docs → trains their own bot →
gets embed code → puts it on their site

You are:        the platform. You never touch their data.
They are:       the customer, doing the work themselves.

MODEL B — Agency / Service (what you just described)
──────────────────────────────────────────────────────
Business pays YOU → gives YOU their documents →
YOU log into your OWN system → create the chatbot yourself →
YOU hand them a working bot / embed code

You are:        the operator. You do the training work.
They are:       a client, not a platform user.
Admin panel:    doesn't need to be separate — 
                your existing dashboard IS your admin panel,

But — important honest question before you simplify
Which one is actually your goal long-term?
If Model B (agency/service):
  → Much simpler to build and launch
  → You are the bottleneck — revenue caps at how many clients YOU can personally onboard
  → No self-serve signup needed, no admin panel needed
  → Remove /register for businesses, keep it just for you

If Model A (self-serve SaaS):
  → Harder to build, but scales without your time being the limit
  → Businesses onboard themselves, 24/7, without you doing anything
  → Admin panel becomes valuable — you're managing potentially hundreds of tenants
  → This is what "SaaS" really means
Both are legitimate businesses. Zapier is Model A. A "we build custom chatbots for restaurants" freelance/agency business is Model B.
